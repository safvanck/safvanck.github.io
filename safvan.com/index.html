Frequently Asked Questions

Have any questions? We're here to help.

Blockchain is a decentralized, digital ledger technology that records transactions across a network of computers. It ensures transparency, security, and immutability of data, making it ideal for various applications beyond cryptocurrencies.

To create a cryptocurrency wallet, choose a reputable wallet provider, download their app or visit their website, and follow the setup instructions. Make sure to securely store your private keys and recovery phrase.

Smart contracts are self-executing contracts with the terms of the agreement directly written into code. They automatically enforce and execute the terms when predetermined conditions are met, without the need for intermediaries.

DeFi refers to financial services and applications built on blockchain networks, operating without traditional intermediaries. It includes lending, borrowing, trading, and other financial activities, all executed through smart contracts.

Blockchain ensures security through cryptographic techniques, decentralization, and consensus mechanisms. Each transaction is encrypted, linked to previous transactions, and verified by multiple nodes in the network, making it extremely difficult to alter or hack.

Public blockchains are open to anyone and fully decentralized, while private blockchains restrict access to authorized participants and are often controlled by a single organization. Public blockchains offer greater transparency, while private blockchains provide more control and privacy.

Businesses can benefit from blockchain through increased transparency, improved security, enhanced traceability, reduced costs, and streamlined processes. It can be applied to supply chain management, financial services, healthcare, and many other industries to increase efficiency and trust.
